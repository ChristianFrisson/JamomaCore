Tutorial 1: Introduction

Jamoma is a proposition for a standard set of guidelines for development of modules in Max. Jamoma consists of two parts: A recommendation and an implementation of those recommendations. Some of the posible benefits that Jamoma might offer are:

  • Encourage reuse of modules from one project to the next.
  • Simplify development in projects involving several artists/developers by offering a common protocol for modules.
  • Simplify the creation of clear, flexible and screen-efficient GUI.
  • A simple and flexible solution for remote control of the states of parameters in the various patches, including cue handling.
  • Possible exchange of patches and modules between Max users in a similar way to how 3rd part externals are currently being made available.

Jamoma modules can roughly be subdivided into 3 categories, control, audio and video, in a similar way to the division between Max, MSP and Jitter. Of course you can imagine modules dealing with audio and video, but let's stick to these three categories for the time being.
